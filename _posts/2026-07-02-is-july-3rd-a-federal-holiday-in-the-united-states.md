## Is July 3rd Recognized as a Federal Holiday?

In the United States, federal holidays are established by Congress and typically result in the closure of non-essential government offices, banks, and some businesses. While July 4th—Independence Day—is a widely celebrated federal holiday, July 3rd is not officially recognized as a federal holiday.

However, when July 4th falls on a weekend, federal observance may shift. For example, if Independence Day lands on a Saturday, the holiday might be observed on Friday, July 3rd. This means that federal employees could have July 3rd off, but this is the exception rather than the rule. For 2024, July 4th falls on a Thursday, so July 3rd remains a regular business day.
