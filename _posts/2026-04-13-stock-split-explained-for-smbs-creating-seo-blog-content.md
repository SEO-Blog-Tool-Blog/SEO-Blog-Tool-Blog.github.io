## What Is a Stock Split?

A stock split occurs when a company increases its number of outstanding shares by dividing each share, which in turn reduces the price per share. However, the overall value of the shareholder’s investment remains unchanged. For example, in a 2-for-1 stock split, shareholders receive an additional share for every share they own, and the price per share is halved.

### Why Are Stock Splits Important?

Stock splits often generate buzz because they can:

- Make shares more affordable for a broader range of investors
- Signal a company’s confidence in its future growth
- Increase the liquidity of the stock

With the rise in stock split announcements, blog topics related to stock splits have become highly searched.
